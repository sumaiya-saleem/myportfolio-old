LEPC Research & Design
Web Content and Design Intern, Peel Halton Workforce Development Group (LEPC), 2018

An eight week internship at the Peel Halton Workforce Development Group (LEPC), to develop an interactive website on Experiential Learning, on the company's new project on Experiential Learning in the Peel Halton region, Project ELPH. Skip to final designs.


About the Project
The goal of the project was educate the different stakeholders on providing Experiential Learning opportunities for High school and University/College Students. The website will work to educate website visitors on what experiential learning is, different ways in which employers can provide experiential learning opportunities to students , the co-benefits of engaging in experiential learning for students and for employers, as well as provide visitors with an informative resource that can help employers connect with different educational institutions across Ontario who are in need of employers willing to onboard internship and co-op students and partake in experiential learning opportunities.
My role as a Web Content Research and Design Intern consisted of two major responsibilities: researching and creating website content and information packages, as well as website design, which you can view here.

Research and Process
For the research phase, I conducted vast amounts of primary and secondary research on labour trends, government funded employment services, experiential learning theories and best practices, and on other topics assigned. Four key stakeholders were identified (Students, Employers, Educators, and Parents) and I conducted user research on each of them, and conducted a meeting with an advisory committee consisting of each of the stakeholders to understand what they would like to learn from and expect in these packages.

Design and Execution
I compiled all research into the five seperate information packages for each of the stakeholders using Adobe InDesign.
